How Verdict Desk actually decides

Every word in your text is weighed against patterns learned from tens of thousands of real and fake articles. Sensational, clickbait-style phrasing — "breaking," "shocking," "watch," "you won't believe" — tends to show up far more often in fake articles, while sourcing language like "according to," named officials, and wire-service dates tend to show up in real reporting. The Verdict Desk doesn't know anything about the world; it only knows what fake and real articles have historically sounded like

That's why it can be wrong — a well-written fake article can sound calm and sourced, and a real story about something wild can sound sensational. When the evidence is too close to call, the Desk says so instead of guessing. Treat every verdict as a starting point for your own judgment, not the end of it.
